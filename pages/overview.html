Overview of Home Energy Options

Solar Energy. Catching some rays.

Solar panels are a popular choice for homeowners looking to reduce electricity costs and decrease their carbon footprint. They convert sunlight into electricity, providing a clean and renewable source of energy for your home.

Learn more about solar energy

Wind energy. Rolling plains and spinning blades.

Small-scale wind turbines can be an effective solution for generating energy in windy areas. These turbines can supplement your home's energy needs, further reducing reliance on non-renewable energy sources.

Learn more about wind energy

Geothermal Energy. Hot springs for a greener tomorrow.

Geothermal heat pumps use the stable temperature of the earth to heat and cool your home more efficiently than traditional systems. While the upfront cost can be higher, the long-term savings and environmental benefits are substantial.

Learn more about geothermal energy

Home energy improvements. More than just a pretty picture.

Improving your home's insulation and upgrading to energy-efficient windows can significantly reduce heating and cooling costs. These improvements help maintain a comfortable indoor temperature year-round, using less energy. Investing in energy-efficient appliances is another way to reduce energy consumption. Look for products with the Energy Star label to ensure you're getting appliances that meet strict energy efficiency guidelines set by the EPA.

Learn more about improving your homes energy efficiency
